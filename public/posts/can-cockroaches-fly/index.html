At 80 degrees, Some Roaches become frequent flyers.¶

Surprisingly, some roaches fly while trying to hide from the sweltering heat. So besides just crawling up the house drains, roaches might fly to get into your home. The most common household roaches (the American cockroaches) do not generally fly and dont regularly use their wings. Further, cold temperatures such as during winter and what you find down drains and sewers prevent them from using their flight muscles. However, if the temperature rises to about 80 degrees and above (like with the summer weather), the American roaches will definitely become regular fliers. The hot weather also tends to increase the roaches metabolism and reproduction capacity, leading to an even higher roach population.
