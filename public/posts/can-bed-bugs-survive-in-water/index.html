Whether bedbugs can survive in water depends on several factors. First, a recently fed bedbug tends to be heavy and takes the shape of a balloon. These two qualities make the bedbugs to sink when exposed to a pool of water. When submerged in water, the bed bug cannot survive for long due to a lack of air.�On the other hand, an unfed bedbug appears flat, oval-shaped, and light in weight. While floating, the bedbugs have access to atmospheric air, which makes them survive. However, you can drown the floating bedbugs by agitating the water with a washing machine or hand.
